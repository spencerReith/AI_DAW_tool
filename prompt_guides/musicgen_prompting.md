# Melody Repurposing Guide
### Transforming a Melody into Any Style Using MusicGen & Prompt Spark

---

## 1. Overview

This guide walks you through the process of taking an existing melody and reimagining it in a new style using **MusicGen's melody conditioning** feature combined with **Prompt Spark** for enhanced prompting. By the end, you'll be able to consistently transform any melody into virtually any genre or mood.

---

## 2. Melody Conditioning with MusicGen

MusicGen's melody conditioning feature allows you to upload reference audio that guides the **melodic and harmonic structure** of the generated music. This is particularly useful for:

- Reimagining existing melodies in different styles
- Creating variations of musical themes
- Maintaining melodic consistency across different arrangements
- Exploring genre transformations of familiar tunes

### Best Practices for Reference Audio

For best results with melody conditioning, your reference audio should:

- Feature a **clear, recognizable melody** without background noise or distortion
- Be **15–30 seconds long**
- Focus on **simple instrumental or vocal melodies** rather than complex multi-layered arrangements

---

## 3. Text-Based Music Generation

The style transformation is driven by your text prompt. A well-crafted prompt gives MusicGen the context it needs to wrap your melody in a new sonic identity.

### 3.1 Crafting Effective Prompts

Effective MusicGen prompts should be descriptive and specific across four dimensions:

| Dimension | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Genre / Style** | "jazz fusion," "ambient electronic," "rock ballad" |
| **Instrumentation** | "electric guitar and bass," "piano and strings," "synthesizers" |
| **Mood / Energy** | "upbeat and energetic," "melancholic," "mysterious" |
| **Production Style** | "lo-fi," "polished studio sound," "live recording feel" |

> **Example prompt:**
> *"Upbeat jazz fusion with electric guitar lead, walking bass line, and crisp drum kit, recorded in a professional studio setting"*

---

### 3.2 Advanced Prompting Techniques

For more sophisticated results, layer in additional descriptors:

1. **Tempo Descriptors** — "fast-paced," "moderate tempo," "slow and contemplative"
2. **Musical Structure** — "verse-chorus structure," "instrumental solo section"
3. **Harmonic Content** — "minor key," "blues progression," "complex jazz harmonies"
4. **Rhythmic Elements** — "syncopated rhythm," "straight beat," "swing feel"

> **Example prompt:**
> *"Cinematic orchestral piece in D minor with soaring string melodies, powerful brass sections, and dramatic percussion, building to an epic crescendo"*

---

### 3.3 Using Prompt Spark

Scenario's **Prompt Spark** can enhance your MusicGen prompts by:

- Adding genre-specific terminology
- Suggesting instrumentation details
- Expanding basic concepts into comprehensive descriptions
- Providing production and mixing descriptors

Use Prompt Spark when your initial style idea feels too vague — it bridges the gap between a rough concept (e.g. *"make it feel cinematic"*) and a fully-formed, generation-ready prompt.

---

## 4. Putting It All Together — Workflow

Follow these steps each time you want to repurpose a melody:

1. **Prepare your reference audio** — Trim to 15–30 seconds, isolate the main melody, remove noise.
2. **Define your target style** — Decide on genre, mood, instrumentation, and production feel.
3. **Draft your prompt** — Use the four dimensions from §3.1 as a checklist.
4. **Enhance with Prompt Spark** — Run your draft through Prompt Spark to add depth and terminology.
5. **Add advanced descriptors** — Layer in tempo, structure, harmony, and rhythm details (§3.2).
6. **Upload to MusicGen** — Enable melody conditioning, attach your reference audio, and paste your prompt.
7. **Iterate** — Adjust the prompt based on results; small wording changes can have a significant impact.

## 5. Quick Reference — Prompt Template

```
[Genre/Style], [Instrumentation], [Mood/Energy], [Production Style],
[Tempo], [Harmonic Content], [Rhythmic Feel], [Structural Notes]
```

**Example:**
```
Ambient electronic, layered synthesizers and soft piano, melancholic and ethereal,
lo-fi production, slow and contemplative, minor key with suspended chords,
gentle pulsing rhythm, fading outro with reverb tail
```
